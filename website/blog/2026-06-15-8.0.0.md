Today we are releasing Babel 8 stable.

It has been almost 8 years since when Babel 7 was [first released](./2018-08-27-7.0.0.md), and we are excited to finally be hear. This is a major milestone for the project, and we are grateful to all the contributors who have made this possible.

The builds tool ecosystem has grown signficantly since then, with new players such as [Oxc](https://oxc.rs/docs/guide/usage/transformer.html) and [SWC](https://swc.rs/), and we are excited to see how Babel 8 will continue to evolve and support the JavaScript community.

Babel 8 is an incremental improvement over Babel 7, trying to minimize the amount of breackage that users have to go to. For those of you that remember the [Babel 5 to Babel 6](https://babeljs.io/blog/2015/10/29/6.0.0) and [Babel 6 to Babel 7](https://babeljs.io/blog/2018/08/27/7.0.0) migrations: don't worry, this time it will be simpler!

Babel 8 comes with almost no new features: everything good in it can already be enabled in Babel 7, and we are mostly switching what behaviors Babel now follows by default.

## Major Breaking Changes

### Babel 8 is ESM-only

Babel now ships as ESM, and requires Node.js 24, 26, or newer.

Node.js 22 and 25 reached end-of-life earlier this year, and will not receive any more security fixes: if you are using any of those versions, or even older ones, we strongly recommend you upgrade to a supported version of Node.js.

### Stop compiling to ES5 by default

In Babel 8, `@babel/preset-env` no longer compiles your code to ES5 by default. Instead, we rely on [Browserslist's `defaults` query](https://browsersl.ist/#q=defaults), which as of today means roughly defaulting to ES2023. Note that this is a moving target, and it will automatically change over time as users update to newer browsers.

Babel still allows you to compile to ES5 (even to ES3, for some features), but you'll need to explicitly define your [`targets`](https://babeljs.io/docs/options#output-targets) in your configuration.

As part of this change, `@babel/preset-env` also defaults to compiling to ESM rather than to CommonJS.

### Deprecate `loose` and `spec` options

Babel has supported for long time `loose` and `spec` options, both in `@babel/preset-env` and in individual plugins. They were used to tweak the balance between spec compliance and output size/performance, but it was often unclear how they were actually changing semantics of compiled code.

In Babel 8, we are _removing_ them from `@babel/preset-env` and _deprecating_ them from individual plugins. Instead, you should use [`assumptions`](https://babeljs.io/assumptions), that let you fine-grained control over what tradeoffs you want to make.

If you are currently using `@babel/preset-env`'s `loose` or `spec` options, we highly recommend you migrate to `assumptions` before migrating to Babel 8 (they have been supported since Babel 7.13.0). You can find the equivalent `assumptions`-based consiguration [in the docs](https://babeljs.io/docs/assumptions#migrating-from-babelpreset-envs-loose-and-spec-modes).

### Extract polyfill injection to separate packages

In 2020 we started the [babel-polyfills](https://github.com/babel/babel-polyfills) project, which allows better control over how Babel injects polyfills while transpiling your code. The original goal was that other polyfill maintianers would be able to implement their own version of the polyfill injector, but in practice `core-js` remained the only one properly supported.

In Babel 8, we are removing the `corejs`/`useBuiltIns` options from `@babel/plugin-transform-runtime` and `@babel/preset-env` in favor of [`babel-plugin-polyfill-corejs3`](https://github.com/babel/babel-polyfills/tree/main/packages/babel-plugin-polyfill-corejs3), which provides a centralized way of controlling `core-js` injection.

We recommend you migrate to this new plugin before updating to Babel 8.

## Other breaking changes

There is a large list of additional breaking changes: most applications will be affected by a few of them. You can find the full migration docs in the [Upgrade to Babel 8](https://babeljs.io/docs/v8-migration) guide.

If you are developing a tool that internally embeds Babel, or if you maintain Babel plugins and presets, you can read the [Upgrade to Babel 8 (API)](https://babeljs.io/docs/v8-migration-api) guide too.
